David Cheriton

Professor of Computer Science

Pioneer in distributed systems and computer networking, early Google investor, and visionary entrepreneur.

Distributed Systems Computer Networking Internet Infrastructure Entrepreneurship

Introduction

David Cheriton is a renowned computer scientist and professor at Stanford University, widely recognized for his pioneering contributions to distributed systems and computer networking. He earned his PhD in Computer Science from the University of Waterloo and joined Stanford's faculty in 1981.

Cheriton is perhaps best known as one of the earliest investors in Google, providing $100,000 in seed funding to Sergey Brin and Larry Page in 1998. This investment, made based on his belief in their revolutionary approach to internet search, eventually grew into one of the most successful investments in Silicon Valley history.

Throughout his career, Cheriton has bridged the gap between academic research and practical implementation, founding several successful technology companies including Granite Systems (acquired by Cisco) and Kealia (acquired by Sun Microsystems). His work has fundamentally shaped modern internet infrastructure and distributed computing systems.

Vinton Cerf

Vice President and Chief Internet Evangelist at Google

Co-designer of TCP/IP protocols and Internet architecture, recognized as one of the "Fathers of the Internet".

Internet Architecture TCP/IP Protocols Interplanetary Internet Digital Communications

Introduction

Vinton Gray Cerf is an American Internet pioneer and one of the principal architects of the modern Internet. He is widely recognized as one of the "Fathers of the Internet" for his crucial role in co-designing the TCP/IP protocols and the overall architecture of the Internet.

Cerf earned his PhD in Computer Science from UCLA in 1972, where he worked on the ARPANET project. Together with Robert Kahn, he developed the Transmission Control Protocol (TCP) and Internet Protocol (IP), the fundamental communication protocols at the heart of the Internet.

Currently serving as Vice President and Chief Internet Evangelist at Google, Cerf continues to shape the future of digital communications. He is actively involved in developing the Interplanetary Internet, a project aimed at creating communication protocols for space exploration.
