About

Professor Nandini Das is a distinguished academic and literary scholar specializing in Early Modern literature and culture at the University of Oxford. Her research focuses on Renaissance studies, travel literature, English Renaissance theatre, and cultural history.

Born in Kolkata, India in 1974, Professor Das initially studied sciences before transitioning to English literature. She has become a leading voice in her field, with particular expertise in Shakespeare studies, Renaissance romance writing, and early travel literature documenting encounters between different cultures.

She currently serves as Professor of Early Modern Literature and Culture at the University of Oxford and Tutorial Fellow at Exeter College, Oxford.

Research Focus

TIDE Project

Travel, Transculturality and Identity in England, c.1550-1700

Professor Das leads this major research initiative exploring early modern travel and cultural exchange. The project examines how travel and encounters with different cultures shaped English identity during the Renaissance period.

Research Methodology

Her work combines literary analysis with historical research, focusing on how texts reflect and shape cultural encounters. She examines travel writing, diplomatic correspondence, and literary works to understand cross-cultural exchanges.
