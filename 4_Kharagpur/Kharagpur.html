Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur

Administrative Changes & Institute-Faculty Dispute

In late 2024, IIT Kharagpur witnessed a major confrontation between the institute's administration and the IIT Teachers' Association (IITTA), leading to significant administrative changes and the replacement of department heads.

Prof. Adrijit Goswami

Former Head, Department of Mathematics

Background

A senior mathematician and well-known faculty member at IIT Kharagpur with significant contributions to the Mathematics department.

Replacement Context

  • Removed from his position as Head of Mathematics Department in December 2024
  • Was among the 86 faculty members who signed the collective representation supporting IITTA leaders
  • Replaced before completing the standard 3-year tenure for department heads

Prof. Niloy Ganguly

Former Head, Department of Artificial Intelligence

Background

Known for his pioneering work in AI systems, computation, and network science. A prominent figure in the AI research community.

Replacement Context

  • Relieved from his position as Head of Artificial Intelligence Department
  • One of the key signatories to the mass representation supporting the teachers' association
  • His removal occurred during the peak of the institute-faculty dispute in late 2024

Prof. Nihar Ranjan Jana

Former Chairperson, Central Research Facility (Life Science Division)

Background

A respected researcher in life sciences with significant contributions to bioscience and biotechnology research at IIT Kharagpur.

Replacement Context

  • Removed as Chairperson of the Central Research Facility (Life Science Division)
  • Joined the group of professors supporting IITTA's stand against the administration
  • His removal came shortly after he signed the collective representation

Background of the Dispute

September 2024: Initial Allegations

  • IIT Teachers' Association submitted a detailed letter to the Union Education Ministry accusing the administration of nepotism and arbitrary faculty recruitment [citation:3][citation:5]
  • The letter criticized the administration for failing to establish a promised multi-speciality hospital on campus [citation:5]
  • Faculty requested the appointment of a new director with "inclusive governance" practices [citation:3]

November 2024: Escalation

  • Administration issued show-cause notices to four IITTA office-bearers [citation:3]
  • 86 faculty members signed a mass petition supporting the IITTA leaders [citation:7]
  • Show-cause notices were subsequently issued to all 86 signatory faculty members [citation:3][citation:7]
  • Faculty threatened hunger strike if demands were not met [citation:7]

December 2024: Department Head Replacements

  • Three department heads were replaced on December 2-3, 2024 [citation:1][citation:5]
  • All three replaced professors were among the 86 signatories [citation:3]
  • None of the replaced heads had completed their standard 3-year tenure [citation:1]

Differing Perspectives

Faculty Perspective
  • Described the removals as "vindictive" and retaliatory [citation:3]
  • Argued the actions aimed to silence dissent and weaken solidarity [citation:3]
  • Stated that the removed heads had not completed their 3-year terms [citation:1]
  • Explored legal options including approaching Calcutta High Court [citation:9]
Administration Perspective
  • Maintained actions were according to rules and regulations [citation:3]
  • Stated administrative positions demand neutrality and non-involvement in organizational disputes [citation:3]
  • Described faculty allegations as "propaganda" and "disruptive" [citation:5]
  • Accused a section of teachers of propagating "collective hateful purpose" [citation:1]

Aftermath and Resolution Efforts

  • Teachers' association approached the Board of Governors seeking intervention [citation:4]
  • Faculty held silent marches and protests on campus [citation:1][citation:5]
  • Some faculty members submitted apology letters, which were accepted by administration [citation:4]
  • Deadline for IITTA office-bearers' response extended to December 13, 2024 [citation:4]
